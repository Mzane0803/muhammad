Again he saith,

One woman, one hearth,  
One name to end the wandering.

Yet by dawn,  
The road hath found his feet.

Again, again  
Yea, again.

---

One day he crieth,  
Let me be held.

The next,  
Let me be free.

Thus the heart churns  
Like a wheel in rain,

Again, again  
Yea, again.

---

Leaves the feast  
Calling it poison.

Returns to it.  
Calling it life.

Curses the chain,  
Then polishes it.

Seeks the rose,  
Then kisses the thorn

Again, again  
Yea, again.

---

Again he saith,

One rose, one soul,  
One sacred rest

Names thyself free,  
Yet serves chains.

He has not yet learned                                                                                                                                                     how to hear what does not call

---

He searches  
Not for things,  
But for the end of searching.

In women                                                                                                                                                                           In world                                                                                                                                                                             In himself 

He has not yet learned                                                                                                                                                     how to hear what does not call
