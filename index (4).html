About

I grew up in Pakistan and moved to Canada to study at Western University. My work sits at the intersection of AI, financial markets, and philosophy — I'm drawn to the questions that don't resolve cleanly.

I write about consciousness, identity, impermanence, and the nature of the self — drawing on Buddhist teachings, neuroscience, physics, and direct experience. I believe the most important ideas live at the borders between disciplines.

"Life is like a call option — you know the downside, but the upside is infinite."

Outside of work, I train Muay Thai and boxing, dance salsa, play poker and violin, and read voraciously across physics, psychology, and philosophy. I speak English and Urdu, and I'm learning French.
